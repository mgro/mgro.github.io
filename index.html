About

I am a resident physician in internal medicine at Charité - Universitätsmedizin Berlin and a junior digital clinician scientist at the Berlin Institute of Health. My research centers around the use of next generation sequencing and epidemiological data to improve our understanding of antibiotic resistance and transmission of Mycobacterium tuberculosis. I am a visiting research fellow at the Department of Biomedical Informatics at Harvard Medical School where I conducted postdoctoral work advised by Prof Maha Farhat. Before that, I graduated with an MD-PhD from the University of Groningen, The Netherlands. My doctoral research took place at the Institut Pasteur Paris (Prof Roland Brosch) where I generated BCG mutants including ESX-1 secretion systems to improve on BCG vaccine efficacy. I was also advised by Stefan Niemann at the Research Center Borstel (Germany) where I studied the genomic diversity of the opportunist Stenotrophomonas maltophilia .
