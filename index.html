About

Physician-scientist and statistician with focus on cardiology, cardiovascular imaging, deep learning, computer vision, and the statistical analysis of large datasets. I majored in statistics at Rice University, obtained my MD at UCSF, and recieved post-graduate medical education in internal medicine and cardiology at Stanford University. I completed a computer science postdoctoral fellowship at Stanford June 2020 and was mentored by Euan Ashley and James Zou.

Starting Fall 2020, I will be starting a research group as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Cardiology at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center. I will be able to recruit 1 - 2 post-doctoral fellows in the general area of computer vision and medical imaging. Specific interests include object detection, tracing, segmentation, and related areas applied to cardiovascular imaging. Highly motivated trainees will work on vision projects and aim to publish their work in leading conferences and journals in both computer vision and medicine (CVPR, ICML, NeurIPS, Nature, NEJM, JAMA, etc).

For technically minded candidates, desired qualifications include strong programming ability in Python, research experience in the area of computer vision with familiarity with PyTorch and Torchvision, experience with CUDA preferred but not required, and mathematical skills with background in EE, CS, math, or related fields.

For medically minded candidates, desired qualifications include a strong work ethic, publication record, and experience working with large datasets. Experience with R, ggplot2, dplyr (tidyverse), and SQL is highly preferred but not required.

Prospective candidates are encouraged to contact me with CV, github link, and brief introduction of interests.
